Maps of Amazônia.

This page is devoted to maps of all kinds pertaining to Amazônia and the history of frontier development.

Amazônia From Space (http://www.dpi.inpe.br/grid/quick-looks)

This site is run by the INPE, and contains many LANDSAT images of the entire Brasilian Amazon. These are the same maps as the ones used by the Brasilian Amazon Deforestation Survey Project.

Area of Boundary Dispute (http://www.lib.utexas.edu/Libs/PCL/Map_collection/americas/ Peru_Ecuador_81.jpg)

At this site is a map that shows an area on the western edge of Amazônia that has been claimed by both Ecuador and Peru for many years. Several armed conflicts have been waged over this strip of land, and thus is proof that nation-state penetration of Amazônia has had major consequences.

The Global Atlas Of Paleovegetation (http://www.soton.ac.uk/~tjms/adams3. html)

This is a site that contains maps of Amazônian climatological patterns between 18,000 years ago and the present. These maps demonstrate both how the Amazon region is capable of changing over time and what type of environment ancient populations might have faced.

Humid Tropical Forest Inventory Project (http://www.bsrsi.msu.edu/)

NASA, the University of New Hampshire, the University of Maryland, and Michigan State University provide interactive image maps of "The Legal Amazon" at this site. LANDSAT images of the region are presented, as is a map that divides Brazilian Amazônia up into its component political departments.

Map of Altar do Chão, Brazil (http://www.city.net/img/tra/mag/map/altard. gif)

A map that shows the layout of a typical town in the region.

Political Map of South America (http://www.lib.utexas.edu/Libs/PCL?Map_collection/americas/ SAmerica_95.jpg)

This is a simple political map of South America. It is included in this listing because the national boundaries of Amazônia and the proximity of the frontier itself to major South American markets are shown.
