Welcome to Archaeotour




This presentation is a generalized demonstration of the stages involved in an average archaeological investigation. For our purposes, we will be investigating a ficticious desertic environment. The image to the left is a tent that will serve as the base of operations for the present archaeological season.
Throughout this tour, we will be observing the processes involved in excavating this desert site at a general level. However, the specific examples of technique and procedure will come from other sites and contexts.




For this season, our research team will be exploring an area of the desert floor near several spring-fed rivers. The main objectives for this year are to find material remains representing a span of several thousand years, map and reconstruct the prehistoric environment, and discover what species of plant and animal were once indigenous to the area.


The first step in the process of answering these questions comes with the reconnaisance and survey of the area to be explored. This is what is called a data universe, and includes all possible areas of exploration within the immediate region. In common parlance, the name of the immediate region would be the site. Now let's attempt to find promising areas for potential finds.
