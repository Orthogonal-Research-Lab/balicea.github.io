Books, Articles, and Other Media.

On this page, viewers will find works about the ecological effects of settlement with a focus on deforestation.

1. Books

Ozorio de Almeida, Anna Luiza and João S. Campari
1996 Sustainable Settlement in the Brazilian Amazon. World Bank, Washington D.C.

This report presents economic arguments about why deforestation has occured, and claims it is mainly due to small peasant settlers. The authors take an analytical approach within the context of a changing frontier that is constantly expanding.

Chibnik, Michael
1994 Risky Rivers: The Economics and Politics of Floodplain Farming in Amazonia. University of Arizona Press, Tucson.

No review done.

Cowell, Adrian
1990 The Decade of Destruction. Hodder and Stoughton, Seven Oaks, Kent, UK.

This book tries to assess damage done by settlement and resource extraction on the frontier. The author also attempts to give solutions for this onslaught.

Dickinson, Robert E.
1987 The Geophysiology of Amazônia: Vegetation and Climate Interactions. John Wiley and Sons, New York.

This resource crtically examines the Amazônian resource base from a geophysiological perspective. The author talks about causes of deforestation, biogeochemical cycles in the tropics, the susceptability of these systems to human intervention, and offers several maps of modeling projects that relate to the research data.

Furley, Peter A.
1994 The Forest Frontier: Settlement and Change in Brazilian Roraima. Routledge Publishers, London.

In this work, changes on the frontier are evaluated in the context of Roraima's change in status from colony to state. Furley also examines the effects goldstrikes, deforestation, and spontaneous settlement have had on this part of the frontier.

Kosinski, Leszek K. ed.
1992 Ecological Disaster in Amazônia. UNESCO publication, Paris.

This report contains a collection of papers that sees Amazônian frontier development as a process of social and economic occupation. The collection also blames conflicting interests for much of the environmental destruction and inequitable distribution of resources in Amazonia.

Wagley, Charles ed.
1974 Man in the Amazon. University of Florida Press, Gainesville.

A collection of papers that is probably one of the best overviews of interaction between people and their environment on the Amazon frontier. This book deals with topics like "Environment and Culture", and "Adaptive Strategies of Frontier Settlement".

2. Articles

Anderson, Anthony B. and Eduiges M. Ioris
1992 Valuing the rainforest: economic strategies by small-scale forest extractivists in the Amazon estatuary. Human Ecology: An Interdisciplinary Journal 20(3): 337-370.

This article presents small-scale extraction on the frontier as a compromise between conservation and environmental destruction. A case study of people's strategies on Combu Island is used to illustrate this point.

Brondizio, Eduardo S., Emilio Moran, Paul Mausel, and You Wu
1994 Intergrating Amazonian vegetation, land use, and satellite data. Bioscience 44(5): 329-339.

A study that uses remote-sensing data from satellites to show deforestation and human ecological trends resulting from frontier development.

Hames, Raymond and Napoleon Chagnon
1979 Protein Deficiency and Tribal Warfare in Amazonia: New Data. Science 203(4383): 910-913.

No review done.

Hames, Raymond
1989 Time, Efficiency, and Fitness in the Amazonian Protein Quest. Research in Economic Anthropology 11: 43-85.

No review done.

Hammond, D. S.
1995 Modern Ticuna swidden fallow management. Human Ecology: An Interdisciplinary Journal 23(3): 335-356.

This article reports how frontier market linkages have changed the farming practices of the Ticuna people in Colombia. The article focuses on the change wrought by a frontier situation on ecologically sensitive lands.

Hiraoka, Mario
1982 Development of Amazônia. Geographic Review 72(1): 94-98.

An overview of several themes including ecological issues in frontier development, and discusses the consequences of colonization. The article also evaluates themes as they apply in Peru, Bolivia, and Brazil.

McGrath, David, Fabio de Castro, Celia Futemma, Benedito Domingues de Amaral, and Juliana Calabria
1993 Fisheries and the evolution of resource management on the lower Amazon floodplain. Human Ecology: An Interdisciplinary Journal 21(2): 167-196.

An article that stresses the importance of the Amazon floodplains as ecologically balanced areas where subsistence fishing thrives. The authors then assess the ecological impact of frontier development by commercial fisheries and ranchers in these ecozones.

Millikan, Brent H.
1992 Tropical deforestation, land degradation, and society: lessons from Rondonia, Brazil. Latin American Perspectives 19(1): 45-72.

This paper focuses on both the social causes of forest destruction and the plight of small farmers. Millikan frames both of these issues in an ecological context and critically examines alternative resource management schemes like ranching on the frontier.

Moran, Emilio
1991 Human adaptive strategies in Amazônian blackwater ecosystems. American Anthropologist 93(1): 361- 382.

Moran (who is also head of the ACT center at Indiana U.) examines areas of the Amazon basin where the local ecosystem can mitigate social and cultural expansion. Moran deals with various human adaptive strategies along the Rio Negro as a case study of a phenomena that could affect frontier development.

Pichon, Fransisco J.
1992 Agricultural settlement and ecological crisis in the Ecuadoran Amazon. Policy Studies Journal 20(4): 662-678.

This article deals with not only the complexity of processes in settlement and deforestation, but also their variability from one locale to another. Pichon also tells why the government, colonists, and industry are willing to turn into a frontier what he considers such a marginal and fragile environment.

Sinclair, Joseph H. and Theron Watson
1923 Explorations in eastern Ecuador. Geographical Review 13(2): 190-210.

A source that chronicles an expedition to a previously unmapped area of the Oriente, Ecuador. The authors also describe the people and landforms encountered while exploring this developing frontier.

Southworth, Frank, Virginia Dale, and Robert U. O'Neill
1991 Contrasting patterns of land use in Rondônia: simulating the effects of carbon release. International Social Science Journal. 43(4): 681-699.

This article examines how changes in land use and deforestation affect social, economic, and ecological conditions on the frontier. The authors also concentrate on the temporal history and spatial patterns of changes in land use.

Wood, Charles H. and Marianne Schmink
1993 Military and environment in the Brazilian Amazon. Journal of Political and Military Sociology 21(1): 81-105.

This article evaluates the role of the military, especially during the critical period of frontier development from 1964 to 1985. The authors also show how this military presence has affected patterns of settlement and land use in the region. Wood and Schmink also give an overview of the theory and method of their own frontier research.

3. Films, Web Sites, Etc.

The Anthropological Center for Training and Research on Global Environmental Change (http://www.indiana.edu/~act/)

The home site for Indiana University-Bloomington's ACT institute, directed by Emilio Moran. The center specializes in studying the human dimension of environmental change, and is currently home to several ongoing research projects.

Boorman, John (Dir.) and Rospo Pallenberg
1985 The Emerald Forest Embassy, Hollywood, Ca.

A film, but no review done.

Conserving Amazonia's Biodiversity. (http://www.wri.org/wri/biodiv/b18-gbs.html)

This site, courtesy of the World Resources Institute in Washington, D.C., contains links to other sites and a story of enviromentalism told from the point of view of the Indigenous People's Organizations.

Cowell, Adrian
1980-1990 (4 programs on 5 cassettes). Banking on Disaster Bullfrog Films, Oley, Pa.

78 minutes of film from the 1980's that documents the opening up of Amazônia's frontier by roadbuilding in Rondônia. Likens the experience to an ongoing human and environmental disaster.

Deforestation and Development in Amazônia. (http://ceps.nasm.edu:2020/CEPSDOCS/AMAZON/ amazon.html)

"Deforestation and Development" engages in a dialouge on what has traditionally happened to deforested land. Also discussed are the planning schemes developed as a response to the destructive forces that are endangering the forest.

Museu Paraense Emilio Goeldi (http://www.museu-goeldi.br)

This is the Portuguese and English language site of a museum in Belem, Para that contains a library and archives pertaining to Amazonian development and scientific research. The museum has been involved in research and publishing for over 100 years. This site also introduces the Ferreira Penna Scientific Station, where many research projects on the Amazonian environment are taking place.
