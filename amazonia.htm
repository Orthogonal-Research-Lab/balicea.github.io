Annotated Amazônia was placed online to give students and researchers information about Anthropological research in the Amazon Basin. Overall though, the main thrust of this site is to inform people about frontier development in the region through space and time. Much of the literature pertaining to European colonization focuses on the Brasilian experience. However, Annotated's resources on frontiers cover the entire Amazon basin because the colonization process has been similar throughout the basin, whether it be in Colombia or Bolivia.




There is, of course, variation within each country's portion of Amazônia. Yet in dealing with variables such as ecology, prehistoric archaeology, and indigenous people, the outcomes seem to be similar. With regards to indigenous populations, political boundaries can become less of an issue than the relations between colonizing agents in general and native groups.




In addition to looking for common themes, I also want to show the variability in colonizing schemes, frontier conditions, and indigenous lifeways. This is to emphasize just how diverse and unique the human experience in Amazônia has been. Including more than one national entity will meet that end, although the goals of peoples and governments throughout the region have actually been similar.
