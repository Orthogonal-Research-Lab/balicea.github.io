Books, Articles, and Other Media.

This page deals with the social and economic issues of settlement and colonization.

1. Books

Almeida, Anna Luiza Ozorio
1992 The Colonization of the Amazon. University of Texas Press, Austin

A book that gives an extensive overview of the Amazon frontier and its colonists mainly in economic terms. Also gives information on settlement patterns and the costs of colonization.

Bunker, Stephen J.
1985 Underdeveloping the Amazon: Extraction, Unequal Exchange, and the Failure of the Modern Nation State. University of Illinois Press, Urbana.

Bunker takes a unique perspective on ecological and administrative problems in Amazônia. Concentrates on the social and ecological costs of an extractive economy that dominate the Brasilian frontier. Also evaluates failures of development projects, both public and private.

Carmin, Robert
1953 Anapolis, Brazil: Regional Capital of an Agricultural Frontier. University of Chicago Press, Chicago.

This is a major research paper in Geography that focuses on frontier urbanization. The subject of study is the fast growing market center on Anapolis, Goias, which is seen as an economic node of the frontier by the author.

Dwyer, Augusta
1990 Into the Amazon: Chico Mendes and the Struggle for the Rainforest. Key Porter Press, Toronto

A book that talks about the labor and land struggles of Brasil's Amazônian frontier. Focuses on topics such as the city of Manaus, the Chico Mendes murder, and the indigenous agency FUNAI's relationship with the Yanomami.

Lisansky, Judith
1990 Migrants to Amazônia: Spontaneous Colonization in the Brazilian Frontier. Westview Press, Boulder, Co.

Lisansky concentrates on the frontier situation in Mato Grosso, and introduces the reader to what are called "spontaneous" settlements. Marginalized by ranching concerns and the government's emphasis on planned settlements, these people and their history are explored in an ethnographic context, both on and off the frontier. Lisansky also discusses settler strategies in the face of little land and hostile public policy.

Mahar, Dennis
1979 Frontier Development Policy in Brazil: Study of Amazonia. Praeger Press, New York.

A book that gives a technical overview mainly in economic terms of development policies and how they have applied to Amazonia. The fiscal incentives of development programs like SUDAM and SUFRAMA are also evaluated in terms of their effectiveness. Several chapters contain a statistical analysis of population, labor, and economy on the frontier.

Meggers, Betty
1971 Amazônia: Man and Culture in a Counterfeit Paradise. Smithsonian Institute Press, Washington D.C.

A Cultural Ecologist looks at limits the lush Amazônian environment had on Pre-Columbian population density and social complexity. Meggers also makes analogies with the contemporary situation on the Amazônian frontier.

Reiss, Bob
1992 The Road to Extrema. Summit Books, New York.

This book contains personal accounts of people in the Amazon and New York to examine how their lives are linked. Reiss places the road from Porto Velho to Rio Branco, Brasil in a continuum from past to future in terms of development schemes and spatial position on the frontier. He also talks about actors on the frontier such as indigenous groups and Forest Police who monitor ranchers.

Schneider, Robert R.
1995 Government and Economy on the Amazon Frontier: World Bank Environmental Paper, Number 11. World Bank, Washington, D.C.

A report for the World Bank that contains data on economic issues that lead to frontier instability. Schneider suggests strategies for "efficient settlement", and how the government can work to attain this.

Sutlive, Vinson H.
1985 The Amazon Caboclo: Historical and Contemporary Perspectives. ed. by Nathan Altshuler, Mario D. Samora, and Virginia Kearns, Department of Anthropology publication number 32, College of William and Mary, Williamsburg, Va.

This is a collection of papers that deal with the Caboclo, or Portuguese-speaking peasant. These papers cover the history of Amazonian colonization in addition to a wide range of contemporary issues of change on the frontier. Papers by notables such as Marianne Schmink and Mario Hiraoka are included with other papers on topics like the transformation of society on the Amazon frontier.

2. Articles

Allen, Elizabeth
1992 Calha Norte:military development in Brazilian Amazônia. Development and Change 23(1): 71-100.

An article that discusses the role of the Brasilian military in Amazônian development. Focuses on the Calha Norte project, a goal of which was to "renew and mark" frontiers.

Bunker, Stephen J.
1984 Modes of extraction, unequal exchange, and the progressive underdevelopment of an extreme periphery: the Brasilian Amazon. American Journal of Sociology 89(5): 1017-1064.

This article focuses on the extraction and export of natural resources has an effect on developmental potential in the Amazon region. The consequences of this frontier underdevelopment are then explained in core-periphery terms.

Galloway, J. H.
1982 Human geography in Brazil during the 1970's: debates and research. Luso-Brazilian Review 19(1): 1-21.

J. H. Galloway discusses the "new geography" and Brasil's geographic heritage as it pertains to Amazônia. Aspects of frontier development such as roadbuilding and the fate of indigenous peoples are dealt with in the Amazônia portion of the paper.

Godfrey, Brian
1990 Boom towns of the Amazon. Geographical Rewiew 80(2): 103-118.

Brian Godfrey sheds light on the hidden truth that Amazônia is home to new, dynamic urban centers such as Maraba and Xinguara in this article. He frames urban development on the frontier in the context of land disputes over ranching, mining, farming, and timbering.

Goza, Franklin
1992 Brazilian frontier settlement: the case of Rondonia. Population and Environment 16(1): 37-61.

An article resting on the assumption that Amazônia's frontier is culturally unique and exemplifies this by studying the situation in Rondônia, Brasil. Goza also asserts that the availability of cheap land determines the "opening" and "closing" of the Rondônia frontier in its history.

Haller, Archibald O.
1982 Socioeconomic regionalization of Brazil. Geographic Review 72(4): 450-464.

Haller attempts to distinguish regions of Brazil on the basis of economics and demographics. This project also seeks to establish 360 microregions determined by homogeneity in each area's ecology. Haller's regional divisions may be more effective at marking boundaries on the frontier than the current political boundaries are.

Kyle, Steven C. and Aercio C. Cunha
1992 National factor markets and the macroeconomic context for environmental destruction in the Brazilian Amazon. Development and Change 23(1): 7-34.

This is an article that criticizes current development policy for treating the Amazon as being devoid of people or long-term economic potential. The authors see this region as an "open" frontier, and seek a model of for sustainable development in Amazônia. Also of note here is their emphasis on studying economic links of both the frontier itself and to the larger Brasilian market.

Pollak, Harrison, Marli Mattos, and Christopher Uhl
1995 Profile of palm-heart extraction in the Amazon estatuary. Human Ecology: An Interdisciplinary Journal 23(3): 357-386.

These authors advocate the harvest of palm-hearts as a renewable resource by frontier farmers. Pollak, Mattos, and Uhl argue that this crop is both sustainable and cost-effective for farms located on this ecologically sensitive frontier. The article also discusses how expansion of palm-hearts can aid in forest management.

Weinstein, Barbara
1985 New views on Amazonian development. Luso-Brazilian Review 22(1): 79-88.

A book review that critiques both Nigel Smith's Rainforest Corridors and Emilio Moran's The Dilemma of Amazonian Development while also asking questions about these books in this light.

3. Films, Web Sites, Etc.

Ancient Raised Field Agriculture: Applied Archaeology in the Bolivian Amazon (http://www. sas.upenn.edu/~cerrickso/applied.html)

This applied archaeological project is based in Llanos de Moxos, Bolivia, and involves constructing raised fields on savanna lands for farming. Based on ethnographic analogy, this experimental program seeks to put this type of Pre- Columbian agriculture back into practice.

Projecto Piaba (http://www.cr-am.rnp.br/FUA/ppiaba.html)

This site evaluates the ecological and social relations involved with ornamental fisheries. The aim of this program is to promote ornamental fish hatcheries and thus keep renewable resources a viable option in the middle Rio Negro basin, Brazil.

Satellite Images: Fires in Amazonia (http://cimiss.ssec.wisc. edu/goes/misc/980317.html)

A site that shows GOES satellite images of fires burning mostly in Roraima, Brasil. Caused by frontier development, these fires burn out of control and actually hamper life in Amazônia.

SHIFT Project (http://www.cr-am.rnp.br/shift/)

This is a study being done near Manaus, Brasil to test sustainable farming and to regenerate spent lands. The project also seeks ways to lessen human impact on the region.
English version also available.
