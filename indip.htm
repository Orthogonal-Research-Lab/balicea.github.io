Books, Articles, and Other Mediums.

This page provides viewers with resources on the ecological effects of settlement, with a focus on deforestation.

1. Books

Chagnon, Napoleon
1974 Studying the Yanomamo. Holt, Rhinehart, and Winston, New York.

This book provides ethnographic data such as geneologies, pictures, settlement patterns, and village relations. The Yanomamo live on the border of Venezuela and Brasil in numerous villages, one of which Chagnon took a complete census of for a section in his book. There are also sections with personal interviews, a chapter on ethnographic film, and a glossary of Sociocultural terminology.

Hemming, John
1981 Colonial Frontier: Plight of the Brazilian Indians. Anti-Slavery Society, London.

A book that deals with the history of contact among six indigenous groups. Hemming also frames the larger history of expansion in Brasil to the need for cheap land, which is in turn leading to the destruction of indigenous groups.

Kane, Joe
1995 Savages. Knopf, New York.

This source is a book that talks about the Huaorani of Ecuador and how their culture is facing the challenges of development and resource exploitation. The most notable set of interrelationships in this book are those involving the Huaorani, the trappings of modernity, and oil development.

O'Connor, Geoffrey
1997 Amazon Journal: Dispatches From a Vanishing Frontier. E. P. Dutton, New York.

Jeffery O'Connor is a film documentarian writing about the gold rush in Amazonia and the relations between settlers and indigenous people. His book concentrates on the situation in Brasil, while also offering many images of the subject matter. O'Connor approaches the subject by talking about resources versus people and their cultural environment.

Sponsel, Leslie E. ed.
1995 Indigenous Peoples and the Future of Amaz么nia: An Ecological Anthropology of an Endangered World. University of Arizona Press, Tucson.

Leslie Sponsel presents a dozen passages from various sources pertaining to ongoing issues facing indigenous populations in Amaz么nia as a result of frontier development. Along with Sponsel's own insights, Archaeological and Physical Anthropological perspectives are included to lend support for historical discussions on ecology and sustainability in the Amazon.

2. Articles

Albert, Bruce
1992 Indian Lands, environmental policy, and military geopolitics, in the development of the Brazilian Amazon: the case of the Yanomami. Development and Change 23(1): 35-70.

This paper illustrates how the military dictatorship's "indigenous policy" affected Yanomami culture and the expropriation of their lands. Albert also evaluates how this type of frontier policy changed if at all during the New Republic from 1985 to 1990.

Frechione, John
1990 Supervillage formation in the Amazon terra firme: the case of Asensona. Ethnology 29(2): 117-134.

This article talks about the Yekuana village of Asensona that developed during the 1960's to take advantage of emerging frontier market links. This village was unusually large for Amaz么nia, but Frechione says that economic conditions and not the environment are responsible for settlement scale in this region.

Stearman, Allyn MacLean
1990 Effects of settler incursion on fish and game resources of the Yuqui. Human Organization 49(4): 373-385.

Stearman's paper targets settlement and deforestation on the frontier as contributing to the destruction of indigenous people. The focus of this study is the Yuqui, who have been confined to missions and can no longer practice their nomadic lifeways because of settler incursions into their territory.

3. Films, Web Sites, Etc.

Arawak Struggles in the Amazon (http://www.hartford-hwp.com/taino/links1.html)

"Arawak Struggles" is a set of links about indigenous groups all along the frontier. The links lead to maps, indices, and photos.

Carpenter, Katherine
1990 In the Name of Progress Santa Barbara Intellimation, Santa Barbara, Ca.

A film about Amaz么nian development, but no review done.

Chagnon, Napoleon, Peter Biella, and Gary Seaman
1997 Yanomamo Interactive: Understanding the Ax Fight Harcourt, Brace, and Company, New York.

An interactive CD-ROM based on the classic ethnographic film "The Ax Fight". Includes clips from the movie along with explanation of the on-screen action and genealogical backgrounds of the Yanomamo involved.

Documentary Educational Resources (http://www.xensei.com/docued/)

This is a company that offers many ethnographic films for sale or rental. Titles include most films in the Yanomamo series, shot by Napoleon Chagnon and Timothy Asch in 1974.

Museu Do Indio (http://www.potiron.com.br/MuseuDoIndio/homepage.htm)

This is the Portuguese language site of Museu Do Indio in Rio de Janeiro. The museum is a privately funded advocate of preserving indigenous heritage, and is geared towards children and adults alike.

Restoration in the Peruvian Amazon: Indigenous Response (http://www.wri.org/wri/biodiv/b25-gbs.html)

This site discusses the HIFCO project near Pucalpa, which is on a frontier highway. The program is designed to help people harvest crops from marginal lands.
