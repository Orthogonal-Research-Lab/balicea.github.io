Books, Articles, and Other Media.

This page gives sources about the historical, political, and ideological contexts of settlement and colonization.

1. Books

Lorimer, Joyce
1989 English and Irish Settlement on the River Amazon. Series 2, Number 171 Hakluyt Society, University Press, Cambridge, UK.

A book that tells the story of English and Irish attempts at settling along the Amazon during the 17th century. In Lorimer's account, the English and Irish were part of a greater European thrust into Amazônia for economic gain. She also shows frontier outposts to be part of this expansion, run by companies that gained concessions from the Spanish and Portuguese crowns.

Margolis, Maxine
1992 The Last New World: Conquest of the Amazon Frontier. Norton, New York.

Maxine Margolis traces development of the Amazon frontier mainly as a process. She cites ideology as being equal to economic factors in impacting the frontier process. This work also deals with policy quandries in the wake of labor leader Chico Mendes' murder.

2. Articles

Anderson, Robin L.
1981 Government directed frontiers in the humid tropics: Pará, Brazil 1870-1920. Agricultural History 55(4): 392-406.

An article that shows how the local government in Pará tried to establish an agricultural froniter adjacent to the growing city of Belém. Also evaluates the eventual failure of this effort due to things like lack of infrastructure. Provides lessons for modern-day Amazônian colonization schemes.

Burns, E. Bradford
1995 Brazil: frontier and ideology. Pacific Historical Review 64(1): 1-18.

E. Bradford Burns divides the frontier in Brasilian history into "official" and "folk". Burns discusses what colonization has meant for people trying to escape and link up with global capitalism. Also describes the frontier as a homogenizing force.

Corliss, William R.
1990 Great ancient civilizations in Amazônia? Ridiculous! Science Frontiers No. 71

Corliss discusses the possibility of large-scale civilizations on the Amazônian floodplain by indigenous groups dating back 7,000 years.

Gibbons, Ann
1990 New view of early Amazônia. Science 248:1488.

A paper that examines the relationship between ancient Amazônian civilizations and those in Central America and the Andean highlands.

Henshell, Janet
1982 Agricultural colonization in Rondônia. Luso-Brazilian Review 19(2): 169-185.

This article introduces five models of frontier settlement for Amazônia instead of just planned and spontaneous. It also accounts for history in affecting types of settlers and settlement on the Amazon frontier.

Hemming, John
1990 How Brazil aquired Roraima. Luso-Brazilian Review 70(2): 295-325.

Hemming covers the early history of Roraima and how Spaniards, Dutch, and English were all involved in early colonization schemes. He talks about the different reasons for colonization, and covers both Portuguese success and how they cooperated with indigenous peoples to help map and define regional frontiers.

Katzman, Martin T.
1978 Colonization as an approach to regional development. Economic Development and Cultural Change 26(4): 709-724.

A study which uses the example of Northern Parana, which is not in Amazônia but is compared to Amazônian colonization and is evaluated in this light by Katzman. This development scheme in particular was built by British capital, and is the most successful colonization program on any South American frontier to date.

Kelly, Brian J. and Mark London
1983 The new frontier. Wilson Quarterly 12(3): 62-76.

An article that focuses on the history and actors behind Amazônian development in Brasil. Government officials, the Catholic church, and people in the cities are all seen as being either for or against development. Frontier development is also portrayed as part of a "grand vision" for many Brasilians in their desire to create a great nation.

Maligo, Pedro
1991 Symbolic Territory: representation of Amazônia in Brasilian literature. Centennial Review 35(2): 229-248.

Pedro Maligo takes into consideration the romanticism contained in much of Amazônian literature. Images fostered by these works have led to gross misrepresentations of the region by outsiders. Maligo also shows Amazônia's frontier as central to ideological debates within Brasil.

3. Films, Web Sites, Etc.

Fordilanda Rubber Plantation (http:vif27.icair.iac.org.nz/Locations/Fordilanda.htm)

This site offers information on Henry Ford's Rubber Plantation in Amazônia, plus the history of the rubber industry in Santarem, Brasil.

Teatro Amazonas (http://www.internext.com.br/demasi/)

Here is a Brasilian site with pictures of the Manaus Opera House for sale, taken by Tullio Demasi, Henrivelto Costa, and Antonio Netto. The Manaus Opera House was built around the turn of the century, and is still in operation as a cultural center.
